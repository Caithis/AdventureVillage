# Quest Economy Risk Notes

## Problem

If the player can freely direct adventurers, quests become too easy to abuse.

## Desired Tension

The player should ask:

```text
Can I afford to intervene?
Will adventurers solve this naturally?
Will the problem grow if I wait?
Is this worth taking money away from buildings, stock, or upgrades?
```

## Intended Loop

```text
Threat grows.
Adventurers may naturally address it.
Player watches risk.
Player may pay to encourage quest focus.
Adventurers respond with higher interest.
If successful, adventurers receive quest reward.
Rewarded adventurers spend in town later.
```

## Long-Term Economy Consideration

The town needs healthy money faucets and sinks.

Possible faucets:
- visitors returning with outside money
- quest sponsor rewards paid to adventurers
- dungeon loot entering the local economy
- kingdom support/renown grants

Possible sinks:
- quest encouragement
- building construction
- upgrades
- stock purchasing
- staff wages later
- resident housing and upkeep
